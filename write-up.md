# Write-up

I designed the tree around three axes: Locus (Victim vs Victor), Orientation (Contribution vs Entitlement), and Radius (Self-Centrism vs Altrocentrism).

The first goal was conversational flow instead of survey design. Each question is short and emotionally realistic for an employee reflecting at the end of the day. I avoided moralizing language and used neutral options so the user does not feel graded.

Axis 1 focuses on agency. Questions ask whether the employee sees events as things happening to them or as situations they can influence. This is grounded in Rotter's Locus of Control and Carol Dweck's Growth Mindset.

Axis 2 shifts from control to contribution. Instead of asking directly about entitlement, I used interaction-based prompts such as helping, teaching, and recognition. This is based on Organizational Citizenship Behavior and Psychological Entitlement.

Axis 3 widens perspective. It moves from personal stress toward team, colleague, and customer outcomes. This uses Maslow's self-transcendence and perspective-taking research.

Branching decisions were kept deterministic using fixed options and rule-based routing. I chose TSV because it is easy to audit and trace manually.

With more time, I would improve summary personalization and create more nuanced middle-path reflections.
